Select Your Study Plan

Each study plan below includes structured time blocks along with flexible periods. You can adjust times based on your personal schedule, energy levels, and commitments.

High School Plans


Level 1 – Beginner High School

Objectives: Build basic study habits, improve organization, and create a consistent routine.

Weekday Schedule (Flexible):

  • 3:30–4:00 PM (Flexible Start): Rest / snack after school
  • 4:00–4:30 PM: Light review of class notes
  • 4:30–5:15 PM: Homework (adjust time if workload is heavier)
  • 5:15–5:30 PM: Break
  • 5:30–6:00 PM: Optional extra study or reading
  • Night (10 mins): Pack bag and update planner

Weekend Plan (Flexible):

  • Saturday (1–2 hrs anytime): Revision of weak subjects
  • Sunday (30–45 mins): Plan week and set goals

Flexibility Tip: If you’re tired, shorten study time and focus only on priority tasks.


Level 2 – Intermediate High School

Objectives: Balance school, activities, and personal time while improving performance.

Daily Schedule (Adjustable):

  • 4:00–4:30 PM: Review notes (or later if you have activities)
  • 4:30–5:30 PM: Homework (extend to 6:00 PM if needed)
  • 5:30–6:00 PM: Break / extracurricular activities
  • 6:00–6:45 PM: Focused study session (test prep)
  • 7:00–7:30 PM (Optional): Practice questions or group study
  • Night (10–15 mins): Planner update

Weekend Plan:

  • Saturday (2–3 hrs, flexible): Deep study or projects
  • Sunday (1 hr): Weekly review + planning

Flexibility Tip: Move study blocks earlier/later depending on extracurriculars.


Level 3 – Advanced High School

Objectives: Prepare for exams, manage workload, and maximize performance.

Daily Schedule (Structured + Flexible):

  • 4:00–5:00 PM: Homework / assignments
  • 5:00–5:30 PM: Break
  • 5:30–6:30 PM: Intensive study session
  • 6:30–7:00 PM: Break / dinner
  • 7:00–8:00 PM: Past papers or practice questions
  • 8:00–8:30 PM (Optional): Review mistakes

Weekend Plan:

  • Saturday (2–4 hrs): Full past paper practice (split into sessions)
  • Sunday (1–2 hrs): Review errors and revise weak topics

Flexibility Tip: Swap evening sessions with morning sessions if you focus better earlier.


Tertiary Plans


Level 1 – Beginner Tertiary

Objectives: Adapt to independent learning and manage time effectively.

Daily Plan (Flexible Blocks):

  • Morning (varies): Lectures/classes
  • 1–2 hrs after classes: Review notes
  • 1–2 hrs (anytime): Read textbooks and summarize
  • 30 mins (night): Organize notes and tasks

Weekly Plan:

  • Sunday (1–2 hrs): Plan assignments and deadlines
  • 2–3 days/week: Extra revision sessions (1 hr each)

Flexibility Tip: Choose study times based on when you feel most productive.


Level 2 – Intermediate Tertiary

Objectives: Balance studies with work and improve efficiency.

Daily Structure:

  • Morning: Lectures or work shifts
  • Afternoon (1–2 hrs): Review notes
  • Evening (2–3 hrs): Assignments/projects
  • Night (Optional 30 mins): Light review

Weekly Strategy:

  • Break assignments into daily tasks (1–2 hrs each)
  • Schedule at least 1 rest day
  • Use time blocks that fit your work schedule

Flexibility Tip: Shift study hours around your job or busiest days.


Level 3 – Advanced Tertiary

Objectives: Handle research, projects, and deadlines effectively.

Daily Schedule (Flexible Professional Style):

  • 8:00–12:00: Lectures / research (adjust if no classes)
  • 12:00–1:00: Break
  • 1:00–3:00: Assignments or writing
  • 3:00–4:00: Rest / personal time
  • 4:00–6:00: Deep work (research/project focus)
  • Night (Optional): Light review or reading

Weekend Plan:

  • Flexible 3–5 hrs: Work on long-term projects
  • 1–2 hrs: Weekly review and planning

Flexibility Tip: Treat your schedule like a workday but adjust hours based on deadlines.
